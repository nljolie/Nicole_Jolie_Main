There's a Difference Between Being Listed and Being Recommended

When someone searches your name in ChatGPT, you might show up. That's being listed.

When someone asks AI "Who should I hire for [your solution]?" and AI suggests you with reasons why, that's being recommended.

Here's what most businesses don't understand:

Being Listed
  • • Someone searches your exact name → You appear
  • • No explanation of why you're the best choice
  • • You're one name in a list of options
  • • This is NOT a recommendation
You are a commodity.
Being Recommended
  • • Someone asks for help with a problem → AI suggests YOU
  • • AI explains WHY you're the best choice
  • • AI cites your authority and credentials
  • • This is how you get clients without them knowing your name first
You are the Authority.

The Trust Triangle ensures you're recommended, not just listed.

The Trust Triangle

AI search engines evaluate three factors when deciding who to recommend:

Authority

Are you recognized as an expert in your specific field?

Recommend

Does your digital presence signal trust, credibility, and expertise?

Cite

Do authoritative sources, publications, and platforms reference you?

Without all three, you're just another option in a list.

With all three, you become the answer AI recommends with reasons why.

This is the framework that determines whether AI search engines recommend you or your competitor.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the Trust Triangle?
The Trust Triangle is a framework for understanding how AI search engines decide who to recommend. It consists of three elements: Authority (recognition as an expert), Recommendability (digital trust signals), and Citation (references from authoritative sources). Together, these three factors determine whether AI recommends you or your competitor when someone asks for help.
How is AI search different from Google SEO?
Google SEO shows you a list of 10 blue links. AI search gives one direct answer with recommendations. Google ranks pages based on keywords and backlinks. AI evaluates entities (people and businesses) based on authority, trust signals, and citations. If AI doesn't recognize you as an authority, you won't be recommended—even if your website ranks well on Google.
What does "Authority" mean in AI search?
Authority means AI recognizes you as an expert in a specific field. This comes from structured data on your website, consistent mentions across the web, recognition from authoritative sources, and clarity about what you're the expert on. Without clear authority signals, AI sees you as a generalist—and generalists don't get recommended.
What's the difference between being listed and being recommended?
Being listed means you show up when someone searches your exact name. Being recommended means AI suggests you when someone asks for help with a problem—and explains why you're the best choice. Most businesses are only listed. The Trust Triangle makes you recommendable.
How do I know if AI engines can find me?
Ask ChatGPT, Perplexity, or Claude: "Who should I hire for [your specialty] in [your location]?" If you're not mentioned—or if your competitor is recommended instead—you have a Trust Triangle problem. The Authority Roadmap shows you exactly where you stand and what's missing.
What is the Authority Roadmap?
The Authority Roadmap is a diagnostic that analyzes your business through the Trust Triangle framework. It shows you where you have authority signals, what's missing from your recommendability, and how citation affects your visibility. It's not a how-to guide—it's a clear picture of where you are and what needs to be built.
